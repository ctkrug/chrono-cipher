About Chrono Cipher

Chrono Cipher is a daily puzzle for people who like a morning brain teaser but have run out of word games to try. Each day it hides a short, famous quote behind a substitution cipher: every letter of the original has been swapped for a different one, and your job is to work out the swaps and read the quote underneath. The same puzzle goes out to everyone on the same calendar day, so you can compare solve times with friends.

How to play

Pick a symbol in the coded message, then choose the plaintext letter you think it stands for. Guess right and every copy of that symbol flips over across the whole document at once, the way solving a real cipher works. Guess wrong and the cell shakes, so you try another letter. Start with the symbols that show up most: in English, E, T, and A do most of the work, so the most common symbol is usually one of them. The frequency panel ranks every symbol by how often it appears, and the "Request hint" button hands you the most useful next letter when you are stuck. Each hint shows up as a yellow square in your shareable result instead of a green one, so leaning on it has a small, visible cost.

Crack the last letter and the file gets stamped DECRYPTED: the quote types itself out with its author and your solve time, and you can copy a spoiler-free emoji grid to share.

FAQ

Is there a new puzzle every day?
Yes. A fresh quote and a new cipher arrive each calendar day, seeded from the date so every player gets the same puzzle.
Do I need an account?
No. There is no login, no server, and no tracking. Your progress is saved in your own browser, so you can close the tab and come back to the same puzzle.
What kind of quotes are used?
Short, well-known lines from history: speeches, mottos, and famous one-liners, each shown with who said it once you solve it.
I'm stuck. What should I try?
Look for one and two-letter words first (A, I, IT, IS, TO), and use the frequency panel to test the most common symbol as E or T. Still stuck? Spend a hint.
Can I play on my phone?
Yes. The keyboard docks to the bottom of the screen and every tap target is sized for thumbs.
